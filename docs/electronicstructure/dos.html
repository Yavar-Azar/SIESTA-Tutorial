Density of states¶

The density of states (DOS) is defined by

\[g(\varepsilon) = \sum\limits_n \langle\psi_n|\psi_n\rangle \delta(\varepsilon-\varepsilon_n),\]

where \(\varepsilon_n\) is the eigenvalue of the eigenstate \(|\psi_n\rangle\).

For a given orthonormal basis

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{rlrl} g(\varepsilon) &= \sum\limits_i g_i(\varepsilon) ,& g_i(\varepsilon) &= \sum\limits_n \langle \psi_n | i \rangle \langle i | \psi_n \rangle \delta(\varepsilon - \varepsilon_n)\\ g(\varepsilon) &= \int\!\mathrm{d}r g(r, \varepsilon),& g(r, \varepsilon) &= \sum\limits_n \langle\psi_n | r \rangle \langle r | \psi_n \rangle \delta(\varepsilon - \varepsilon_n) \end{array}\end{split}\]

using completeness of the selected basis sets

\(1 = \sum\limits_i | i \rangle\langle i |\) and \(1 =\int\!\mathrm{d}r |r\rangle\langle r|\).

\(g_i(\varepsilon)\) is called the projected density of states (PDOS), and \(g(r, \varepsilon)\) the local density of states (LDOS).

From above equations it can easily be shown that integration of DOS below the Fermi energy gives total number of electrons (N)

\[\int\!\mathrm{d}\varepsilon\, n_F(\varepsilon) g(\varepsilon) = N\]

and

integrating of the LDOS multiplied by a Fermi distribution gives the electron density

\[\int\!\mathrm{d}\varepsilon\, n_F(\varepsilon) g(r, \varepsilon) = n(r)\]

Note

In above definition k-dependence of eigenfunctions dropped for simplicity, for more accurate definition, one can refer to “Quantum Chemistry of Solids: The LCAO First Principles Treatment of Crystals” by Robert A. Evarestov

\[ N=2\sum\limits_{i=1}^M \int\limits_{-\infty}^{\varepsilon_F}\frac{1}{V_B}\int\limits_{BZ}\theta(\varepsilon-\varepsilon_i({\bf k}))d{\bf k} =\int\limits_{-\infty}^{\varepsilon_F}g(\varepsilon)d\varepsilon = \sum\limits_i\int\limits_{-\infty}^{\varepsilon_F}g_i(\varepsilon)d\varepsilon\]
